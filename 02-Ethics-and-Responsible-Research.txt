Ethics and Responsible Research

Having access to public information does not mean you should ignore privacy or ethics. Good investigators understand the difference between gathering information and abusing it.

Before starting any investigation, make sure you understand the laws in your country. Privacy laws, data protection regulations, and acceptable use policies vary depending on where you live.

Never use OSINT to harass, stalk, threaten, or expose someone without a legitimate reason.

Remember that information found online is not always accurate. Fake social media accounts, outdated websites, and incorrect public records are common.

Always verify information by checking multiple independent sources.

Another important concept is operational security, often called OPSEC. Every website you visit collects some information about you, including your IP address, browser type, and sometimes your location.

Using separate accounts and browser profiles for research helps protect your personal identity while keeping investigations organized.

Best Practices

• Stay within the law.
• Respect people's privacy.
• Verify every important finding.
• Keep your personal accounts separate from research accounts.
