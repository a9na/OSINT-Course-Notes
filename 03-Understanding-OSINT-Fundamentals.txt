Understanding OSINT Fundamentals

Public information exists almost everywhere. The challenge is knowing where to look and how to connect different pieces together.

Some of the most useful OSINT sources include search engines, social media platforms, public government databases, business records, archived websites, maps, and technical information such as domain registrations.

Each source provides only part of the story. A social media profile may reveal hobbies, while LinkedIn may reveal employment history. Public records can provide addresses, and company websites may reveal employee email formats.

The goal is to combine information from different places until a clear picture begins to form.

A simple investigation often follows these steps:

1. Identify your starting point.
2. Search multiple sources.
3. Compare the information.
4. Look for connections.
5. Record your findings.
6. Verify everything before drawing conclusions.

Avoid relying on a single website. The strongest conclusions come from information that appears consistently across multiple independent sources.

Things to Remember

• One source is rarely enough.
• Patterns are often more valuable than individual facts.
• Organization makes investigations much easier.
